HISTORY
-------

古墳時代

Kofun Period

堺の打刃物の歴史は５世紀頃に作られた世界最大の古墳と言われている仁徳天皇陵の構築にまでさかのぼります。

The history of Sakai's edged tools dates back to the construction of the Nintoku Emperor's mausoleum, which is said to be the world's largest tumulus, built around the 5th century.

最大全長８４０m 最大幅６５４mの巨大な古墳は構築に１５年以上の歳月をかけて建造されたと言われています。

This enormous tumulus, with a maximum length of 840 meters and a maximum width of 654 meters, is said to have been constructed over a period of 15 years or more.

当時 土を耕したり穴を掘るための鋤やクワが大量に必要になり、それらを作る為に日本中から鍛冶職人が集められ堺に住み着いたと伝えられています。

At that time, a large number of plows and hoes were needed for tilling the soil and digging holes, so blacksmiths from all over Japan were gathered and settled in Sakai to make these tools, according to legend.

戦国時代

Sengoku Period

戦国時代の堺は自由都市として盛大に栄えていたと言われています。

Sakai is said to have prospered greatly as a free city during the Sengoku period.

ポルトガルから種子島に伝来した火縄銃が堺に持ち込まれると優れた鍛冶の技術で瞬く間に日本で有数の火縄銃の産地となりました。

When matchlock guns, brought from Portugal to Tanegashima, were introduced to Sakai, it quickly became one of the leading production centers of matchlock guns in Japan, thanks to its excellent blacksmithing techniques.

堺製の火縄銃の品質はときの戦国武将達からも絶大な支持を受けており、かの戦国武将の織田信長が生涯こよなく愛した火縄銃も堺製だったと言われいます。

The quality of Sakai-made matchlock guns received tremendous support not only from the warlords of the Sengoku period but also from the famous warlord Oda Nobunaga, who was said to have had a deep affection for Sakai-made matchlock guns throughout his life.

江戸時代（天保年間頃）

Edo Period (Around the Tenpo era)

江戸時代に入ると次第に火縄銃の需要は減っていき堺の火縄銃は実用品から装飾を楽しむ形へと移り変わっていく事になります。

As the Edo period progressed, the demand for matchlock guns gradually declined, and Sakai's matchlock guns shifted from practical use to being enjoyed as decorative items.

生産数も激減しているさなかポルトガルから持ち込まれたタバコが人々の間で広がり始めました。

Amidst the sharp decline in production, tobacco, introduced from Portugal, began to spread among the people.

同時にタバコの葉も国内で栽培されるようになり葉を刻む為の包丁を堺の職人達が作り始めたのです。

At the same time, tobacco leaves started to be cultivated domestically, and the craftsmen of Sakai began making knives to cut the leaves.

これが後の堺刃物の起源になると言われています。堺製のタバコ包丁は非常によく切れて長持ちする事が理由で当時の江戸幕府が政府専売品として堺極の刻印で日本全国に販売するほどだったと伝えられています。

This is said to be the origin of Sakai blades. Sakai-made tobacco knives were highly regarded for their sharpness and durability, to the extent that the Edo shogunate at the time sold them nationwide with the Sakai symbol as a government monopoly product.

現代

Modern Times

時代の流れと共にタバコの生産が機械化されていき堺のタバコ包丁は必要とされなくなりました。

As the times progressed and tobacco production became mechanized, the demand for tobacco knives in Sakai diminished.

それまでタバコ包丁を作っていた職人達はその経験と技術を生かして料理用の包丁を作り始めたのです。

The craftsmen who had been making tobacco knives began utilizing their experience and skills to make kitchen knives.

堺刃物の特徴は分業制と言われて鍛冶、刃研ぎの職人がそれぞれの仕事を極める為に生涯の時間を費やしています。

Sakai blades are characterized by a division of labor, where blacksmiths and blade sharpeners dedicate their lifetime to mastering their respective crafts.

お互いが切磋琢磨する事により他産地に類をみない高品質の包丁が生まれているのです。

Through mutual competition and improvement, Sakai has been able to produce high-quality knives that are unmatched by other regions.

昭和５７年に堺打刃物は伝統的工芸品に指定されその品質は日本のみならず世界にも広く認められています。

In 1982, Sakai blades were designated as traditional crafts, and their quality has been widely recognized not only in Japan but also around the world.

堺から世界へ

From Sakai to the World

堺で生まれた打刃物は今や海を渡り欧米や中国、韓国、豪州、東南アジアなど世界３０カ国以上の国に輸出されるようになりました。

Edged tools born in Sakai are now exported to over 30 countries worldwide, including Europe, the United States, China, Korea, Australia, and Southeast Asia.

妥協なく鍛え上げ磨き上げられた包丁は国境を超えて多くの人々を魅了し今後も世界中の人々から注目される存在になると確信しています。

These knives, forged and meticulously polished without compromise, have captivated many people beyond borders, and I am confident that they will continue to be recognized and appreciated by people worldwide.




PROCESS
-------

Preparing for Blade Making - Fire Starting 1:
Preparation for forging the base material for the knife. Using coke, the temperature in the furnace is rapidly raised to 1000 degrees. The heated steel is shaped and prepared for the next process of steel attachment.
火づくり１
包丁の元となる地金を鋼と鍛接する為の準備。
コークスを使い炉の温度を一気に1000度にまで上げていきます。
赤く熱せられた鋼の形を整えて次の鋼付けの工程へと進みます。

Steel Attachment - Fire Starting 2:
A traditional Sakai blade-making technique called "boil-fusion". The steel is heated to over 1000 degrees and bonded together using borax, boracic acid, iron oxide, and other materials. After bonding, it is heated again in the furnace and thoroughly hammered to ensure complete adhesion.
火づくり２（鋼付け）
沸かし付けとも呼ばれる堺打刃物伝統技法。
1000度以上まで熱せられた地金に硼酸、硼砂、酸化鉄などを使い鋼を貼り合わせていきます。
貼り合わせた後、再度炉の中で熱しハンマーで叩きながら完全に接着させていきます。

First Shaping - Fire Starting 3:
Once the base material and steel are fully fused, the knife is gradually shaped closer to its final form using a belt hammer. Care must be taken not to overheat the knife during this process, as excessive heat can cause the carbon, an important element for sharpness, to dissipate. The craftsmen meticulously forge each knife while carefully managing the temperature.
火づくり３（先付け）
地金と鋼が完全に結合したらベルトハンマーを使いながら徐々に包丁の形に近づけていきます。
この際に包丁の温度を高めすぎると切れ味の重要素でもある炭素が逃げてしまい切れ味の良い包丁が出来上がりません。
職人達は常に温度管理と向き合いながら１本の包丁を丁寧に鍛え上げていきます。

Resting - Fire Starting 4:
The finished knife is allowed to rest for a while to stabilize its composition. This period is also used to check for any scratches or joint imperfections in the knife.
火づくり４（寝し）
打ち上げた包丁の成分を安定させる為にしばらく寝かしておく。
同時に包丁に傷や接合ミスがないかをチェックする。

Trimming and Shaping:
The excess material is cut away to shape the knife according to its final design. This work is done meticulously by the craftsmen's hands, without relying on mechanization.
断ち回し
打ち上がりの包丁を形に合わせて余分な場所を切り取っていく作業。
機械化される事なく職人の手の感覚で丁寧に切っていきます。

Rough Hammering:
By repeatedly hammering the cooled base material, the molecules become finer, resulting in increased sharpness. This process also involves creating a hollow (ura-suki) on the backside of the knife.
荒たたき
冷めた地金を何度も叩く事により分子が細かくなり切れ味が増すと言われています。
同時に裏側にくぼみ（裏スキ）を付ける作業も行います。

Quenching 1 - Clay Coating:
A thin layer of clay is applied to the entire blade during the quenching process, also known as the critical heat treatment process, to ensure uniform temperature distribution. It also helps to suppress bubbles that may form during water or oil cooling, allowing for rapid and controlled cooling.
焼き入れ１（泥塗り）
包丁の命とも言う焼き入れの際に刃全体に均一に温度が伝わるようにする為刃全体に薄く泥を塗る。
また、水や油で冷却する際に発生する気泡を押さえて素早く冷却する目的もあります。

Quenching 2 - Cooling:
After drying the applied clay, the blade is heated to around 800 degrees and rapidly cooled. Temperature control relies on the experience and skills of the craftsman, as it is not mechanized. Quenching is a highly concentrated task performed solemnly in the dim light of the evening. The use of pine charcoal, known for its smooth temperature rise, is a distinctive feature of Sakai blades.
焼き入れ２（冷却）
刃に塗った泥を乾かした後、800度前後まで熱し一気に冷却する作業。
温度管理は機械化されておらず職人の経験と技術が最も重要となります。
焼き入れは非常に集中力が必要な作業で夕刻の薄暗い中で厳かに行われます。
堺打刃物の焼き入れは温度がなめらかに上がると言われている松炭を使う事も特徴とされています。

Tempering:
After the quenching process, the knife is hardened but lacks flexibility, making it unsuitable for use. To give the knife the necessary flexibility, it is heated to around 200 degrees and slowly cooled. This process creates a well-balanced knife with both hardness and flexibility.
焼き戻し
焼き入れ作業で硬さを持った包丁は粘りがなくそのままでは使う事が出来ません。
粘りを持たせる為に再度200度位

に加熱した後自然に冷ましていきます。
この作業により硬さと粘りを持った良い包丁が生まれます。

Straightening:
After the quenching and tempering processes, the knife reaches the final stage of the forging process. It involves checking for slight distortions or scratches before being handed over to the blade sharpening craftsman. Any slight distortions are carefully corrected.
ひずみ取り
焼き入れと焼き戻しを終えた包丁は鍛冶工程の最終段階へと辿り着きました。
刃研ぎ職人に渡る前に僅かな歪みや傷をなどを確認する作業です。
僅かな歪みも見逃すことなく修正していきます。

Rough Sharpening:
This is the initial sharpening process done by the blade smith. Using a self-made tool called a "kanna," the blade is sharpened on a rotating grindstone. The knife's condition is carefully assessed, and the work proceeds slowly.
荒研ぎ
鍛冶職人から送られてきた包丁の最初の研ぎになります。
研ぎ棒と呼ばれる自作の道具を使い回転砥石で研いでいきます。
包丁の状態をしっかり見極めながらゆっくりと作業を進めます。

Final Sharpening:
After completing the rough sharpening, further refining is done to make the blade truly sharp. This delicate process requires the utmost skill of the craftsman, as over-sharpening cannot be undone.
本研ぎ
荒研ぎを終えた後、実際に切れるようにする為に更に薄く研いでいく作業。
研ぎすぎた包丁は元に戻せないので職人の技量がもっとも試される工程です。

Buffing:
To give the blade a glossy finish, a rotating buffing wheel with polishing cloth is used. Starting with coarse buffing and gradually transitioning to finer buffs, the knife is finished with a radiant, beautiful appearance.
バフ仕上げ
刃に光沢を持たせる為に回転バフと呼ばれる研磨布を当てていく。
初めは荒く徐々に細かくバフを変えていき最終的には光輝く美しい包丁に仕上げていきます。

Etching (Decorative Sharpening):
A mixture of grinding stone powder and kneaded clay is applied to rubber or wood, then rubbed onto the blade. This important process creates the distinctive wave pattern on the blade, and the specific types and quantities of the powder blend remain a closely guarded secret.
ぼかし（化粧研ぎ）
砥石の粉を粘土状に

なるまでコネたものをゴムや木片につけて刃に擦りつけていきます。
波紋を付ける大事な作業であり配合する粉の種類と量は美しさを引き出す為の肝でありその中身は門外不出とされています。

Final Polishing:
The knife, after the etching process, is meticulously finished by hand polishing. This step determines the sharpness of Sakai blades, as the craftsmen bring out the best potential in each individual knife while carefully assessing its condition. This process involves changing the types of grindstones used for each task and requires a significant amount of time and effort.
仕上げ研ぎ
ぼかし作業を終えた包丁を最終的に手研ぎで仕上げていく工程。
堺刃物の切れ味を決める作業で包丁一本一本の状態を見ながら最高のポテンシャルを引き出していきます。
作業ごとに砥石の種類を変更していく作業でもあり手間と時間のかかる作業です。

Completion:
After the forging and blade sharpening processes are finished, the handle is attached, and the knife is considered complete. Sakai blades are made through a division of labor, with craftsmen in each field striving together, resulting in knives of the highest quality. Please try holding a Sakai Takayuki blade, carrying on the 600-year tradition of Sakai blades.
完成
鍛冶、刃研ぎを終えた包丁はハンドルを取り付けると完成です。
堺打刃物は分業制で成り立っており、それぞれの分野でお互いが切磋琢磨する事により最高品質の包丁が生まれます。
６００年の堺打刃物の伝統を受け継いだ堺孝行刃物をぜひ一度お手に取って下さい。
